Video #1: What is Binary, Digital Data and Abstraction?

  • Binary - number system with only 2 numbers: 0 and 1. 1 means on, and 0 means off.

  • Circuits in processors are made of billions of transistors. (transistor means tiny switch which is activated by signals it receives. 1 means on, 0 means off.)

  • 1s and 0s can represent anything. The computer does a certain function according to context. For example, you press A (the computer sees it as 0 and 1s) but it prints an A for us. Let's say we were playing a game, now the computer interprets it as going left.

  • Abstractions- reduces complexity, hides unnecessary details, helps us focus on the main idea.

  • Analog - a mechanism, device, or technology that represents data by measurement of a continuous physical variable.

  • The use of digital data to approximate real world analog data, is an example of abstraction.

  • To represent the smoothness of a piece of analog data with binary, we do sampling, or measure the values of analog data and regular intervals. Sampling allows for analog data to be turned into digital data.
